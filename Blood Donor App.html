About Section

This website provides information about the importance of blood donation and how you can get involved.

  • Blood Collection: Blood banks organize and conduct blood donation drives to collect blood from voluntary donors. They may also accept directed donations from family members or friends for specific patients in need. Proper donor selection, screening, and evaluation are essential to ensure the safety of both donors and recipients.
  • Blood Testing: Once blood is collected, it undergoes extensive testing to identify blood type (A, B, AB, or O) and Rh factor (positive or negative). Additionally, blood is screened for infectious diseases, such as HIV, hepatitis B and C, syphilis, and others, to prevent the transmission of infections through transfusions.
  • Blood Processing and Storage: After testing, blood is processed into various components, such as red blood cells, platelets, and plasma, to meet specific medical needs. Each component has a different shelf life and storage requirements, and the blood bank must manage inventory carefully to prevent wastage and shortages.
